# Finite Toy Model for the Co-Emergence Thesis

## Motivation

Section 3.6 and Open Problem 2 of the co-emergence paper pose a two-part question:

1. Does the self-consistency map F with Lorentzian phases produce complex-valued fixed points?
2. Does marginalizing the complex fixed point over a bipartition produce a density matrix with quantum coherences (complex off-diagonal elements), not just classical probabilities?

A positive answer would demonstrate that the mechanism proposed in Section 3.6 is operative: Lorentzian self-consistency can produce quantum coherences in subsystem density matrices. A negative answer would mean the framework reorganizes quantum mechanics but does not derive it.

## Model

### Configuration space

S = S_R × S_C with |S_R| = |S_C| = 2, giving N = 4 elements. Each element σ_{rc} represents a "smooth structure" labeled by region (r) and complement (c).

### State

ψ ∈ ℂ⁴, L²-normalized: Σ|ψ_{rc}|² = 1. L² normalization (not L¹) is essential — it treats ψ as an amplitude, enabling density matrices via partial trace rather than merely classical marginalization.

### Mean-field curvature

R_{rc}(ψ) = h_{rc} + α_R · P_r + α_C · Q_c + β · |ψ_{rc}|²

where:
- h_{rc} is the intrinsic curvature of smooth structure (r,c) — the "external field"
- P_r = |ψ_{r0}|² + |ψ_{r1}|² is the region marginal
- Q_c = |ψ_{0c}|² + |ψ_{1c}|² is the complement marginal
- β · |ψ_{rc}|² is the Hartree self-coupling (back-reaction on own curvature)

### Why the external field and Hartree term are needed

The original plan used only marginal couplings: R_{rc} = R₀ + α_R·P_r + α_C·Q_c. This curvature is *separable* in r and c, which means:
1. The uniform state ψ = (1/2, 1/2, 1/2, 1/2) is always a fixed point (all marginals equal)
2. The weights factorize: w_{rc} = f(r)·g(c), forcing the fixed point to be a *product state*
3. Product states have pure reduced density matrices (purity = 1, entropy = 0)
4. The model is trivially classical regardless of θ

The fix requires two physically motivated terms:
- **External field h_{rc}:** Different smooth structures have different intrinsic Einstein-Hilbert actions. This is not an assumption — it's a consequence of smooth structures being geometrically distinct. The non-uniform h breaks the permutation symmetry among configurations.
- **Hartree term β|ψ_{rc}|²:** The curvature at a smooth structure depends on that structure's own weight. This is the standard self-consistent back-reaction — the same mechanism that makes the SCE self-consistent at Level 2.

### Weight function

w_{rc}(ψ) = exp(γ · R_{rc}(ψ))

where γ = -1 + iθ. The real part (-1) suppresses high-curvature configurations (Boltzmann-like). The imaginary part (iθ) adds Lorentzian phases from the action functional.

### Self-consistency and observables

- Fixed point: ψ* = F(ψ*) = w(ψ*)/‖w(ψ*)‖₂
- Reduced density matrix: ρ_R = Tr_C(|ψ*⟩⟨ψ*|)
- Quantum test: Im(ρ_R[0,1]) ≠ 0
- Convergence criterion: ‖ρ(F(ψ)) - ρ(ψ)‖_F (phase-invariant)

### Parameters

| Parameter | Symbol | Default | Meaning |
|-----------|--------|---------|---------|
| External field | h | [1.0, 0.7; 0.5, 1.2] | Intrinsic curvature per smooth structure |
| Region coupling | α_R | 0.5 | Mean-field coupling within region |
| Complement coupling | α_C | 0.3 | Mean-field coupling within complement |
| Hartree coupling | β | 0.4 | Direct self-coupling |
| Lorentzian phase | θ | 1.0 | Signature parameter (0 = Riemannian) |

## Results

### Main finding: POSITIVE

The Lorentzian oscillatory structure (e^{iθR}) survives self-consistency and produces quantum coherences in subsystem density matrices. The Riemannian damping structure does not, because there are no phases to survive.

### Riemannian case (θ = 0)

```
ρ_R = [0.484  0.459]     |Im(ρ[0,1])| = 3.7e-17 (machine zero)
      [0.459  0.516]     Purity = 0.922, Entropy = 0.245 bits
```

The fixed point is *real-valued* (up to a global phase). The reduced density matrix has real off-diagonal elements — classical correlations between region and complement, but no quantum coherences. The state is entangled (purity < 1) but the entanglement is classical.

### Lorentzian case (θ = 1)

```
ρ_R = [0.484        0.420+0.016i]     |Im(ρ[0,1])| = 1.6e-02
      [0.420-0.016i  0.516       ]     Purity = 0.853, Entropy = 0.402 bits
```

The fixed point has *non-trivial complex phases* that differ between components. The reduced density matrix has complex off-diagonal elements — genuine quantum coherences. The purity drops (more entanglement) and the entropy increases.

### Key observation

Both cases have the same |ψ*|² distribution (same occupation probabilities). The *amplitudes* differ: Riemannian fixed points are real, Lorentzian fixed points carry configuration-dependent phases. These relative phases are invisible in the full state (global phase freedom) but become observable through the partial trace — exactly the mechanism the paper proposes.

### Phase diagram

Sweeping θ from 0 to 3:

| θ | |Im(ρ₁₂)| | Purity | Entropy (bits) |
|---|-----------|--------|----------------|
| 0.0 | 0 | 0.922 | 0.245 |
| 0.6 | 0.010 | 0.896 | 0.307 |
| 1.2 | 0.019 | 0.825 | 0.459 |
| 1.8 | 0.029 | 0.727 | 0.642 |
| 2.4 | 0.040 | 0.625 | 0.811 |
| 3.0 | 0.050 | 0.546 | 0.932 |

Quantum coherence grows monotonically with θ. Entanglement entropy also increases. There is no phase transition — the onset is smooth, which is expected for a finite system.

### Spectral analysis

Both Riemannian and Lorentzian fixed points are stable:
- Spectral radius ≈ 0.85 < 1 (contraction)
- Spectral gap ≈ 0.50 (rapid convergence)

The Banach contraction property from Level 2 of the full paper is reproduced here.

### Diagnostic: uniform external field

With h = (1, 1, 1, 1) and β = 0, the model recovers the trivial uniform fixed point with purity = 1 and no coherences, confirming that the external field and Hartree term are what break the symmetry.

## Interpretation

### What the model demonstrates

1. **Lorentzian phases survive self-consistency.** The nonlinear fixed-point equation F(ψ*) = ψ* could have selected real solutions even with complex weights — it does not. The oscillatory structure e^{iθR} is propagated through self-consistency into configuration-dependent relative phases in ψ*. The mechanism is: Lorentzian signature → complex weight → complex fixed point → complex density matrix. Self-consistency doesn't *generate* the phases from nothing; it *preserves* them from the weight function into the fixed point.

2. **Phases → coherences via partial trace.** The complex relative phases between ψ*_{r0} and ψ*_{r1} survive the partial trace over the complement, producing complex off-diagonal elements in ρ_R. This is precisely the mechanism proposed in Section 3.6.

3. **Riemannian → classical.** When θ = 0, the weights are real, the fixed point is real (up to global phase), and the partial trace produces a density matrix with only real entries. There are no phases to preserve, so no quantum coherences appear.

4. **The quantum/classical boundary is the Lorentzian/Riemannian boundary.** The presence or absence of complex off-diagonal ρ_R elements tracks whether γ has an imaginary part.

### What requires caution

1. **The phases come from γ, not from topology.** The Lorentzian coupling γ = -1 + iθ is the action's oscillatory weight e^{iS}. The model demonstrates that self-consistency propagates these phases into subsystem coherences, but it does not demonstrate that topology generates the phases. In the full theory, the Lorentzian action on each smooth structure would provide the phases; in the toy model, they are an input parameter (θ).

2. **The external field h is not derived.** The model assumes different smooth structures have different intrinsic curvatures. In the full theory, this would follow from the Einstein-Hilbert action on each smooth structure, but in the toy model it is a parameter choice. The result is robust to the specific values of h (tested in parameter sweeps) but requires h to be non-uniform. This is itself a structural prediction: the co-emergence mechanism *requires* that smooth structures be distinguishable by their actions.

3. **The Hartree term β is a modeling choice.** The direct self-coupling β|ψ_{rc}|² is physically natural (back-reaction) but its inclusion and magnitude are not derived from the axioms. Setting β = 0 with non-uniform h still produces coherences (the external field alone breaks the symmetry), so β enhances but is not required for the positive result.

4. **N = 4 is minimal.** The tensor product structure S_R × S_C with |S_R| = |S_C| = 2 is the smallest configuration space that supports a partial trace. Larger systems might behave differently, particularly regarding the coherence magnitude and the smoothness of the θ-transition.

5. **The coherence magnitude is small.** |Im(ρ[0,1])| ≈ 0.016 at θ = 1, compared to |ρ[0,1]| ≈ 0.42. The quantum correction is a ~4% effect on the off-diagonal element. Whether this is "enough" quantum mechanics depends on what the full theory produces — this toy model establishes that the mechanism is operative, not its magnitude.

6. **Complex off-diagonal elements ≠ full quantum mechanics.** A density matrix with complex entries is necessary but not sufficient for quantum mechanics. The Born rule (trace rule for probabilities), the composition rule for subsystems, and interference in measurement outcomes are all additional structure. The 2×2 system is too small to test any of these. The model demonstrates one ingredient, not the full package.

## Implications

### What the model shows

The finite truncation demonstrates that the mechanism proposed in Section 3.6 is operative: Lorentzian self-consistency produces quantum coherences in subsystem density matrices that are absent in the Riemannian case. Specifically:

- **Open Problem 2 part (1):** Self-consistency with Lorentzian coupling produces complex-valued fixed points. The phases come from the Lorentzian action (γ = -1 + iθ) and are preserved, not destroyed, by the self-consistency loop.

- **Open Problem 2 part (2):** Marginalizing the complex fixed point produces a density matrix with quantum coherences, not merely classical correlations.

### What the model does not show

This confirms one link of the co-emergence thesis in a minimal setting. It does not confirm the thesis as a whole. Open questions:

- **Scaling to larger N.** Does the coherence survive or grow as Sm(M) becomes large?
- **Born rule and full QM structure.** Does the density matrix satisfy the complete algebraic structure of quantum mechanics, beyond having complex entries?
- **Derivation of h from topology.** The external field should be computable from the Einstein-Hilbert action on each smooth structure. This is a Level 2 calculation the toy model bypasses.
